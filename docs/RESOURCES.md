# Learning Resources

This document provides a comprehensive list of resources that have informed this codebook, including books, websites, papers, and tools. These materials are recommended for deeper understanding and continued learning in deep learning for biology.

> **Note**: This codebook uses **PyTorch** exclusively. However, we include references to other frameworks (TensorFlow, JAX, etc.) for educational purposes and broader learning.

## üìö Books

### Deep Learning Fundamentals

1. **"Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville**
   - The definitive textbook on deep learning
   - Available online: https://www.deeplearningbook.org/
   - Covers mathematical foundations and modern architectures

2. **"Neural Networks and Deep Learning" by Michael Nielsen**
   - Excellent introduction with interactive visualizations
   - Free online: http://neuralnetworksanddeeplearning.com/
   - Great for beginners

3. **"Dive into Deep Learning" by Aston Zhang, Zachary C. Lipton, Mu Li, and Alexander J. Smola**
   - Interactive book with executable code
   - Available at: https://d2l.ai/
   - Covers PyTorch, TensorFlow, and MXNet

4. **"Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning" by Christopher Bishop**
   - Strong mathematical foundation
   - Classic reference for probabilistic models
   - Essential for understanding ML theory

### Bioinformatics and Computational Biology

5. **"Bioinformatics Data Skills" by Vince Buffalo**
   - Practical guide to computational biology tools
   - Covers Unix, Python, R, and data processing
   - Essential for working with biological data

6. **"Biological Sequence Analysis" by Richard Durbin, Sean Eddy, Anders Krogh, and Graeme Mitchison**
   - Probabilistic models for sequences
   - Classic text on HMMs and sequence alignment
   - Mathematical yet accessible

7. **"An Introduction to Systems Biology: Design Principles of Biological Circuits" by Uri Alon**
   - Network motifs and systems-level thinking
   - Bridges biology and computational approaches
   - Excellent for modeling biological systems

8. **"Computational Genomics with R" by Altuna Akalin**
   - Practical guide to genomic data analysis
   - Free online: https://compgenomr.github.io/book/
   - R-focused but concepts apply broadly

### Machine Learning for Biology

9. **"Machine Learning in Bioinformatics" by Yanqing Zhang and Jagath C. Rajapakse**
   - Survey of ML methods in biology
   - Covers various application domains
   - Good overview of the field

10. **"Deep Learning for the Life Sciences" by Bharath Ramsundar, Peter Eastman, Patrick Walters, and Vijay Pande**
    - Practical guide using DeepChem
    - Drug discovery and molecular modeling focus
    - Code examples included

## üìÑ Key Research Papers

### Foundational Deep Learning

1. **"ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks"** (AlexNet)
   - Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton (2012)
   - Kickstarted the deep learning revolution

2. **"Attention Is All You Need"** (Transformer)
   - Vaswani et al. (2017)
   - Introduced the transformer architecture
   - https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762

3. **"Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition"** (ResNet)
   - He et al. (2015)
   - Skip connections for very deep networks
   - https://arxiv.org/abs/1512.03385

4. **"Generative Adversarial Networks"**
   - Goodfellow et al. (2014)
   - Framework for generative modeling
   - https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2661

### Deep Learning for Biology

5. **"Deep learning for computational biology"**
   - Angermueller et al. (2016)
   - Molecular Biology of the Cell
   - Comprehensive review of DL in biology

6. **"A primer on deep learning in genomics"**
   - Zou et al. (2019)
   - Nature Genetics
   - Excellent overview with examples
   - https://www.nature.com/articles/s41588-018-0295-5

7. **"Opportunities and obstacles for deep learning in biology and medicine"**
   - Ching et al. (2018)
   - Journal of The Royal Society Interface
   - Critical analysis of challenges

### Sequence Analysis

8. **"Predicting the sequence specificities of DNA- and RNA-binding proteins by deep learning"**
   - Alipanahi et al. (2015)
   - Nature Biotechnology (DeepBind)
   - CNNs for motif discovery

9. **"Basset: learning the regulatory code of the accessible genome with deep convolutional neural networks"**
   - Kelley et al. (2016)
   - Genome Research
   - DNase-seq and chromatin accessibility

10. **"The Enhancer RNA landscape of human cells"**
    - Yin et al. (2020)
    - Nature Communications
    - Regulatory genomics with deep learning

### Protein Structure and Function

11. **"Highly accurate protein structure prediction with AlphaFold"**
    - Jumper et al. (2021)
    - Nature
    - Breakthrough in structure prediction
    - https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-021-03819-2

12. **"ProtTrans: Towards Cracking the Language of Life's Code Through Self-Supervised Deep Learning"**
    - Elnaggar et al. (2021)
    - Transformers for protein sequences
    - https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.06225

13. **"Unified rational protein engineering with sequence-based deep representation learning"**
    - Alley et al. (2019)
    - Nature Methods
    - UniRep for protein embeddings

### Drug Discovery

14. **"Molecular graph convolutions: moving beyond fingerprints"**
    - Kearnes et al. (2016)
    - Graph neural networks for molecules
    - Journal of Computer-Aided Molecular Design

15. **"Automatic chemical design using a data-driven continuous representation of molecules"**

16. **"Low data drug discovery with one-shot learning"**

### Single-Cell Analysis

    - Lopez et al. (2018)

18. **"Single-cell RNA-seq denoising using a deep count autoencoder"**
